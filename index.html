Advancing Sustainable Agriculture in Bohol

The Bohol Seed Network is a community-driven initiative focused on developing resilient agricultural systems. Through strategic upcycling, localized seed libraries, and circular economy practices, we provide practical, tech-voc aligned training to ensure long-term food security and climate resilience for local farming communities.

Community-Led

Seed Sovereignty

We are dedicated to safeguarding genetic diversity by establishing municipal seed libraries. By preserving heirloom and open-pollinated vegetable varieties, we protect local farmers from seed dependency and reinforce regional agricultural resilience.


Focus Area: Vegetable Seed Production NC III standards and regional adaptation.

Strategic Upcycling

Bioponic Succession Systems

Our low-cost, stagnant bioponic models are designed specifically for non-electric, rural, and resource-limited spaces. We creatively upcycle post-consumer plastics into high-performance cultivation vessels that utilize organic, biologically active nutrient mixtures.


Focus Area: Affordable, zero-electricity hydroponics using organic-based inputs.

Circular Economy

Biochar & Soil Amendments

We actively combat agricultural waste by processing municipal and farm bio-waste into rich, biochar-enriched organic soil amendments. This system builds long-term soil microbiology, increases soil water retention, and locks carbon into the earth.


Focus Area: Repurposing bio-waste substrates for sustainable crop production.
