Why We Save Seeds

More Than Conservation— This is a Revolution of the Soil.

Just like the ancient traditional practices across indigenous lands, Bohol's agricultural wealth doesn't lie in industrial lab-made hybrids, but in the resilient genetic heritage passed down by our ancestors.

By keeping these open-pollinated strains alive, we guard our local food sovereignty against corporate monopolies, adapt natively to shifting climates, and honor the deep spiritual bond connecting the Boholano farmer to the land.

The Biological Treasure of Bohol

Featured Heirloom Strains

These varieties have been meticulously rescued, multiplied, and adapted across decades by Bohol’s native seed keepers.

Tubers / Root Crops

Ubi Kinampay

Extremely Rare

The legendary sacred purple yam of Bohol. Known for its distinct exquisite aroma and deep violet flesh, historically revered in local cultural heritage rituals.

Origin: Jagna & Mabini, Bohol
Grains / Maize

Calape Native Corn

Stable Supply

A rugged, open-pollinated white flint corn heavily resilient against pests and dry island microclimates. Traditionally milled into staple rice-corn grits.

Origin: Calape, Bohol
Vegetables / Legumes

Boholano String Bean

Endangered

A traditional long-pod pole sitaw cultivar passed down organically through generations. Exceptional heat-tolerance during dry season cycles.

Origin: Sierra Bullones, Bohol
